Detail Page 2: Cycling Safety Tips

Cycling in the Netherlands is a wonderful way to get around, but it comes with its own set of unwritten rules and safety guidelines. Whether you're a newcomer or just trying to blend in with the locals, here are some essential tips to keep you safe and confident on the road:

1. To Helmet or Not to Helmet?

In the Netherlands, helmets are not commonly worn by locals, and there is no rule that says everyone needs to wear a helmet. However, if you're new to cycling or want to be extra cautious, it's your safety, after all, and there's no shame in taking extra precautions as you adjust to Dutch cycling culture. Besides, if you see people riding road bikes on the street, they will subconsciously wear helmets because they are faster and more dangerous. So if you also ride a road bike, it is best to wear a helmet.

2. Walk Your Bike Through Busy Areas

If you are cycling through a crowded area, it's best to get off your bike and walk it along with you. Cycling in these areas is unsafe, and it is easy to hit pedestrians and cause accidents.

3. Keep the Pace

When cycling in Dutch cities, you’ll notice that locals tend to ride at a brisk pace. Try to keep up with the flow of traffic to avoid being continually passed, which can be frustrating. If you’re not quite there yet, don’t worry—just do your best to maintain a steady speed and stay to the right so others can pass easily.

4. Don’t Stop Abruptly

Dutch cyclists are used to riding in close proximity to each other, so suddenly braking without warning can cause chaos. Always be aware of your surroundings and try to stop gradually when needed. Sudden stops for no apparent reason won’t be appreciated by fellow cyclists!

5. No Rushing at Intersections

It might be tempting to sneak ahead at intersections when you think you can make it before the light changes, but this is dangerous. Dutch cyclists are skilled, but the roads can be chaotic. Play it safe and wait your turn—it's not worth the risk.

6. Beware of the Rain

Cycling in the rain is very common in the Netherlands. A big part of the reason is that the weather in the Netherlands is unpredictable. Don’t use a umbrella when cycling, the wind can easily knock it over, making your ride more dangerous. Instead, get some waterproof gear like a good raincoat to stay dry.

7. Don’t Overload

In the Netherlands, it’s common to carry almost anything on your bike’s luggage rack or in saddlebags, but be careful about how much stuff you’re carrying. An overweight bicycle will be very difficult to ride, and loss of balance, causing the bicycle to wobble. Make sure your load doesn’t block other cyclists from passing, and always secure your items properly.

8. Preventing Bike Theft

Bike theft is a frequent issue in the Netherlands, so it’s essential to lock your bike securely. Use a heavy D lock to secure your frame to an immovable object.
