Cycling Rules

In the Netherlands, cyclists must use designated lanes, signal turns, yield appropriately, and follow local traffic signs and etiquette for safe and lawful cycling.

1. General Cycling Rules in the Netherlands

  1. Always use the bike lane when one is available. (Usually bike lanes are maroon)
  2. Stay on the right side of the bike lane and overtake other cyclists from the left. If there is a two-way bike lane, you donâ€™t have to go to the right side of the road, but if the two-way bike lane ends and becomes a one-way bike lane, you must follow the rules and go to the right side.
  3. If you are overtaking someone, ring your bell to let them know you are passing.
  4. You are allowed to ride side-by-side with one other cyclist, but no more than two.
  5. While there are traffic lights specifically for cyclists, you must stop at every red light.
  6. Cycling on sidewalks is not allowed.
  7. In addition to the official cycling rules, there are many things to keep in mind while riding. By following Dutch cycling etiquette, you can avoid unnecessary trouble for yourself and others.

2. Right-of-Way Rules for Cyclists

  1. At any intersection without signs, shark teeth markings, or traffic lights, you must give way to vehicles coming from your right.
  2. Trams always have the right of way.
  3. If you are turning left or right, you must yield to all road users who are going straight, including pedestrians.
  4. Emergency vehicles with flashing lights and sirens (such as police cars, fire trucks, and ambulances) always have the right of way.
  5. Pedestrians have the right of way at crosswalks.

3. Cyclists at Roundabouts

  1. You must yield to any vehicles already on the roundabout when you are approaching.
  2. Once you are on the roundabout, you have the right of way over incoming vehicles.
  3. When exiting the roundabout, signal your intent by extending your right arm.
