For newcomers to the Netherlands, adjusting to cycling in the Netherlands can be challenging. Knowing the rules of the road, following safety guidelines, and maintaining your bike are essential to navigating Dutch cities safely and confidently.

If you don't know the rules, you'll make mistakes even when you're trying to get through a simple traffic light. This website will cover the key aspects of cycling in the Netherlands, and provide practical advice on rules, safety, and bike maintenance, so you don't get scared by making some silly mistakes on your first bike ride.

The Netherlands is recognized as a world leader in cycling. With 22.8 million bicycles shared by around 17 million people (Dutch government), cycling is part of Dutch culture. With dedicated bike lanes on most streets, traffic lights for cyclists, and extensive infrastructure, the Netherlands is undoubtedly a cyclist's paradise.

In the Netherlands, cycling is more than just a form of transportation; it's an integral part of daily life. Whether it's getting to work, school, or exploring the scenic countryside, cycling is a natural choice, and the high cost of public transportation has made cycling even more popular.
